% Abstract: AI4MATH 2026 workshop submission.
\begin{abstract}
Lean 4 with Mathlib ships strong general automation (\texttt{aesop},
\texttt{simp}, \texttt{linarith}, \texttt{polyrith}, \texttt{nlinarith},
\texttt{positivity}, \texttt{measurability}, \texttt{bound},
\texttt{gcongr}). It does not ship a statistics-domain hammer.
We present \texttt{pythia}, a Lean 4 tactic library that closes
statistical-domain goals via five registered tactics:
\texttt{pythia} (general dispatcher),
\texttt{stats\_ineq} (concentration and tail-bound inequalities),
\texttt{prob\_simp} (probability normalization rewrites),
\texttt{anytime\_valid} (Ville-bound closer for confidence sequences),
and \texttt{z3\_check} (Z3 oracle with linarith reconstruction in the
CoqHammer style of \citet{czajkakaliszyk2018coqhammer}).
The library ships with extensible attribute-driven registries
(\texttt{@[stat\_lemma]}, \texttt{@[stats\_ineq]},
\texttt{@[prob\_simp]}) and a named aesop ruleset \texttt{Pythia}.
A library of approximately twelve modules covers anytime-valid
confidence sequences \citep{howard2021time,waudbysmith2024estimating},
matrix concentration scaffolds \citep{tropp2012usermatrix,lieb1973}, e-detectors
\citep{shinramdasrinaldo2024,ramdasgrunwaldvovkshafer2023},
queueing primitives, control Lyapunov bounds, and the binary
Bretagnolle-Huber inequality. Every shipped theorem is
axiom-clean against the standard Lean kernel set
($\texttt{propext}$, $\texttt{Classical.choice}$, $\texttt{Quot.sound}$). Hard structural Mathlib gaps are routed to
the Aristotle automated theorem prover
\citep{harmonic2025aristotle} as an oracle, with kernel-checked
reconstruction inside Lean and a refusal policy for vacuous-truth
closures. The library is pinned to Lean 4.28 + Mathlib v4.28.0 and
released open-source. Measured on a small subset of the FormalAVS
benchmark \citep{anonymous2026formalavs}, Aristotle as oracle closes
problems unreachable by hand-Mathlib alone. Full numbers are
reported in the dataset paper.
\end{abstract}
