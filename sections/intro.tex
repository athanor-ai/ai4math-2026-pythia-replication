\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Lean 4 with Mathlib ships strong general-purpose proof automation:
\texttt{aesop} for forward search, \texttt{simp} for rewriting,
\texttt{linarith} and \texttt{polyrith} for linear and polynomial
arithmetic, \texttt{nlinarith} for non-linear arithmetic,
\texttt{positivity} for sign goals, \texttt{measurability} for the
$\sigma$-algebra fragment, \texttt{bound} for monotone composition,
\texttt{gcongr} for generalized congruence
\citep{moura2021lean,mathlib2020community,mathlib2025}. Statistical
proofs route through these general tactics. Each statistical lemma
typically requires by-hand decomposition into a chain of
\texttt{simp} sets, \texttt{linarith} hypotheses, and Mathlib lemma
selection. There is no domain hammer for statistics.

% TODO: tighten this paragraph; pin one-sentence claim of the gap.
The closest design template is CoqHammer
\citep{czajkakaliszyk2018coqhammer}: an external ATP (Vampire,
E, CVC4) discharges goals with kernel-checked reconstruction inside
Coq. Sledgehammer
\citep{paulsonblanchette2010threeyears,blanchettesledgehammer2011}
plays the same role in Isabelle/Higher-Order Logic (HOL). Lean 4 lacks a comparable
Sledgehammer-class tool for general proof, and lacks a
statistics-domain hammer specifically. We do not address the general
case in this paper. We address the statistics-domain case.

\paragraph{Contribution.}
We present \texttt{pythia}, a Lean 4 tactic library for statistical
proof automation. \texttt{pythia} ships five registered tactics, one
named aesop ruleset, three attribute-driven registries, and a
twelve-module proof library covering anytime-valid confidence
sequences, matrix and scalar concentration, e-detectors,
queueing, control, and information-theoretic divergences. The
shipped library is axiom-clean against
$\{\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice}, \texttt{Quot.sound}\}$
and pinned to Lean 4.28 + Mathlib v4.28.0. Hard structural goals are
routed to Aristotle \citep{harmonic2025aristotle} as an oracle, with
kernel-checked reconstruction inside Lean.

\paragraph{Case study (vacuous-truth catch).}
% TODO: shorten to 4-5 lines. Cite project ID redacted.
On 2026-04-26 the Aristotle prover closed a candidate theorem in our
\texttt{wald\_wolfowitz\_optimal} development by routing through a
contradictory premise: a Bool partition combined with a log-boundary
constraint forced both $\alpha \geq 1$ and $\alpha < 1$, closing the
goal vacuously. The closure was kernel-checked. The theorem statement
was the bug. \texttt{pythia}'s discipline (axiom-clean only, refuse
vacuous closures, audit hypotheses) caught the issue and forced a
restatement to the two-measure form. We treat this kind of catch as a
first-class library responsibility, not as a separate review pass.
